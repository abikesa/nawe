*What is to see in Shona?*
In **Shona**, "to see" is **kuona**. It carries a layered meaning: - **Literal seeing** (with the eyes). - **Spiritual vision** (dreams, intuition, foresight). - **Understanding** (perceiving truth or essence). Unlike Zulu’s heavy **ukubona** (existential encounter) or Luganda’s lighter **okulaba** (mechanical noticing), Shona’s **kuona** balances **physical sight** with **mystical insight**. It’s less raw than Zulu, less polished than Swahili, but deeply tied to **knowing** and **divination**. Example: - *Ndakaona zviroto.* ("I saw dreams" — implying prophetic vision.) --- **Brutal one-liner:** > **Kuona is seeing with both eyes and soul.**
